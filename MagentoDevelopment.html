Frequently Asked Questions

We’ve put together a comprehensive F.A.Q. on the topic of Magento Web Design. We hope this will help you decide whether this is a valid solution for you. For any other questions not covered here, feel free to send us an email at info@xhtmlchop.com OR contact us.

  • 1. Do we need to sign some agreements before embracing the Magento eCommerce Solution?
    • A

      If you use the Community Edition, no. However, the Enterprise Edition will require you to sign a Service Level Agreement, or SLA.

  • 2. I plan to use Community Edition for my online store. Will I receive any bug-fixing or customer support from Magento?
    • A

      Yes! Bug tracking and fixing services from Magento is currently available no matter which version or edition you’re planning on using. The team will give more priority to the Enterprise users for bug fixing, but that doesn’t mean they limit their support to Community users. Both versions come with adequate support.

  • 3. Where can I get extensions and other add-ons?
    • A

      Visit http://www.magentocommerce.com/magento-connect

  • 4. What features does Magento support?
    • A

      PSD to Magento have a number of essential features, including:

      • Marketing promotions
      • Marketing tools
      • Analytics and reporting
      • Search Engine Optimization
      • Site Management
      • Catalog management
      • Catalog browsing
      • International Support
      • Checkout and Payment
      • Shipping
      • Customer Service
      • Customer Accounts
      • Order management

      Essentially, Magento is a one-stop shop for everything related to setting up an eCommerce website that your users will trust and want to use.

  • 5. What payment gateways are possibly with Magento?
    • A

      Why not ask Magento? They include a feature list (http://www.magentocommerce.com/product/features) and you can also check out Magento Connect (http://www.magentocommerce.com/magento-connect) as well.

  • 6. How much time do you need to convert one page design into a Magento theme?
    • A

      Typically, the time frame is 1 page of PSD to a Magento theme within 4-5 business days. There are a lot of other factors that will affect your turnaround time, such as the complexity of the design, any customization you might require, and extension integration. But if you work with us, we’ll give you an estimate based on your parameters, not our fixed schedule.

  • 7. Does the price of working with you include the installation of extensions?
    • A

      Yes! Installation of the extensions is included in the cost of your project. However, if we need to customize an extension, we will have to charge an additional customization fee, so keep that in mind. If you do decide to work with us, your project manager will tell you all about the cost of your individual customization before initiating the project. We don’t “surprise” you with any hidden costs.

  • 8. Where can I see a demo?
    • A

      Check out the demo at http://www.magentocommerce.com/demo

  • 9. What licenses does Magento use?
    • A

      If you use the Magento Community Edition, it’s licensed under the Open Software License (OSL) v3.0, which is an open source certified license. You can read more about Magento’s license and policy at http://www.magentocommerce.com/license. The premium Magento Enterprise Edition process is licensed under a commercial license.

  • 10. Why open source?
    • A

      A recent report by O'Reilly Research on usage of Open Source in the enterprise, provides a number of reasons for the rise and meteoric growth of Open Source products, including: Agility and scale, reduced vendor lock-in, quality and security, cost, and innovation.

  • 11. What features does Magento support?
    • A

      There is an entire list of features at http://www.magentocommerce.com/product/features -- that’s the best place to go to see the latest features and what to expect form a Magento eCommerce solution.

  • 12. What materials do I need to provide to Magento design integrators and developers?
    • A

      The most obvious is a Photoshop (*.psd) source file with separate layers. But for best results, you should be read to provide PSD design sketches for the key pages like the home page, the category page, the product details page, and the cart page. A design sketch for a 404 page is also recommended.

      Don’t forget about other file types, such as the Favicon image. Some web designers ignore the Favicon image, but if you leave it out, your website will run the default Magento Favicon after the store launch. That’s no way to brand! You can easily use http://www.favicon.cc as the service to generate a favicon that represent your website.

      It will also be important to provide source files (*.ttf) of non-standard fonts that you plan on using. This is very important because the fonts on your page reflect highly on the professionalism and style of your company. Fonts that are licensed for web usage would work best—you can find these at http://code.google.com/webfonts or http://www.fontsquirrel.com. These fonts are licensed to use on the web.

      What’s more:

      • Generating a placeholder image for your products is a good idea, to avoid the Magento default placeholder.
      • A store logo in a printable format
      • Use a “logo_print.gif” – a logo that can be on all of your printed materials like invoices.
      • “Logo_email.gif” is for emails.

      A good way to go about this is simply to ask your designer for printer-and-email-friendly versions of your logo.

  • 13. How should I choose a company for PSD to Magento conversion service?
    • A

      When all materials are ready and design is approved, find a company that develops a PSD to Magento theme conversion. Look for certified, in-house developers, find official Magento partners, and be sure to check the portfolio and testimonials to ensure that the company provides quality service.

  • 14. What is the result of quality PSD to Magento conversion service?
    • A
      • W3C-valid
      • CSS, XHTML
      • SEO-friendly
      • Should support Google Rich Snippets standard
      • Cross-browser capable (Firefox, Chrome, Explorer, Opera, and Safari)
      • Professional jQuery/Protytype implementation
      • The Magento theme should be upgrade-friendly for future use
